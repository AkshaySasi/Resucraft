DO's and DON'Ts while Crafting your Resume

DO's
  1. DO tailor your resume for each job application to highlight the most relevant skills and experiences.
  2. DO use a clean and professional resume template provided by the resume building website to ensure a polished appearance.
  3. DO use clear and concise language to effectively communicate your qualifications and achievements.
  4. DO proofread your resume carefully to catch any spelling or grammar errors. Utilize the built-in spell check feature of the resume building website.
  5. DO include keywords from the job description to optimize your resume for applicant tracking systems (ATS) when applicable.
  6. DO quantify your achievements and use specific metrics to showcase your impact and results.
  7. DO include relevant and up-to-date contact information, such as your name, phone number, email address, and LinkedIn profile.
  8. DO use action verbs to start your bullet points and showcase your accomplishments and responsibilities.
  9. DO include a professional summary or objective statement at the beginning of your resume to provide a concise overview of your skills and career goals.
  10. DO focus on the most recent and relevant work experience and education.
DON'Ts
  1. DON'T include personal information such as your marital status, age, or photographs unless explicitly requested by the employer or if it's customary in your specific industry or country.
  2. DON'T use unprofessional email addresses. Create a dedicated email address that incorporates your name or initials for job applications.
  3. DON'T include irrelevant or outdated work experience or education that does not contribute to your qualifications for the job.
  4. DON'T overcrowd your resume with excessive details or unnecessary information. Keep it concise and relevant.
  5. DON'T use unprofessional or informal language. Maintain a professional tone throughout your resume.
  6. DON'T lie or exaggerate your qualifications, skills, or experiences. Be truthful and accurate in representing yourself.
  7. DON'T rely solely on the resume building website's templates. Customize and personalize your resume to make it unique and tailored to your specific background and career goals.
  8. DON'T forget to proofread and edit your resume before finalizing it. Mistakes can detract from your professionalism and attention to detail.
  9. DON'T neglect to include relevant skills and keywords from the job description. This can improve your chances of getting past ATS and catching the attention of recruiters.
  10. DON'T forget to save and export your resume in a compatible file format (e.g., PDF, Word) to ensure it can be easily viewed and shared with potential employers.
